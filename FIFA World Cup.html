FIFA World Cup 2014 News

The FIFA World Cup 2014 was held in Brazil from June 12 to July 13, 2014, marking the country's second time hosting the prestigious tournament since 1950. This global football event featured 32 teams from around the world and was celebrated for its vibrant atmosphere, passionate fans, and dramatic matches. The tournament reached its peak when Germany defeated Argentina 1–0 in the final at the iconic Maracanã Stadium in Rio de Janeiro, thanks to a late extra-time goal by Mario Götze. A standout moment of the tournament was Brazil’s shocking 1–7 semifinal loss to Germany, which stunned fans worldwide. The event was hosted across 12 cities, including São Paulo, Brasília, Fortaleza, and Salvador, each offering a unique glimpse into Brazil’s culture and beauty. With memorable performances by players like James Rodríguez, who won the Golden Boot, and thrilling matches that kept fans on edge, the 2014 World Cup is widely remembered as one of the most exciting and emotional editions in football history.

Destinations

  • Maracana Stadium – Rio de Janeiro
  • Arena Corinthians (also known as Arena de Sao Paulo) – Sao Paulo
  • Estádio Nacional Mane Garrincha – Brasilia
  • Mineirao Stadium – Belo Horizonte
  • Arena Castelao – Fortaleza
  • Arena Fonte Nova – Salvador
